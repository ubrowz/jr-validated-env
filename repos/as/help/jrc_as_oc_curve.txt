
What this script does:
       This script plots the Operating Characteristic (OC) curve for any attributes
       acceptance sampling plan defined by a sample size n and acceptance number c.
       Use it to visualise how a plan performs across the entire range of lot quality,
       or to evaluate a plan that has been specified externally (e.g. from a standard
       such as ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 or ISO 2859).

       The OC curve shows the probability of accepting a lot (Pa) as a function of
       the true fraction defective (p) in the lot. A steeply-sloping OC curve
       indicates a plan that discriminates well between good and bad lots. A flat
       curve indicates poor discrimination. At p = 0, Pa = 1.0 (every perfect lot
       is accepted). As p increases, Pa decreases.

       By default, the binomial distribution is used: each unit in the sample is
       independent and has probability p of being defective. If the lot size N is
       provided via --lot-size and n/N > 10%, the hypergeometric distribution is
       used instead, which is more accurate for small lots. The hypergeometric
       accounts for the fact that sampling without replacement from a finite lot
       reduces the variability of the number of defectives found.

       Optional --aql and --rql flags annotate the OC curve at those quality levels
       with vertical lines and labels showing the exact Pa value.

Description: Plot the OC curve for an attributes acceptance sampling plan (n, c).
             Outputs a table of p vs. Pa and saves a PNG to ~/Downloads/.

Usage: jrc_as_oc_curve <n> <c> [--lot-size N] [--aql value] [--rql value]

Arguments:
       n               Sample size (positive integer).
       c               Acceptance number (non-negative integer, must be < n).
       --lot-size N    Lot size. If provided and n/N > 0.10, uses the hypergeometric
                       distribution; otherwise uses the binomial.
       --aql <value>   Optional: mark AQL on the OC curve with a blue vertical line.
       --rql <value>   Optional: mark RQL on the OC curve with a red vertical line.
       -h | --help     Show this help message.

Output — Terminal:
       Table of fraction defective p vs. probability of acceptance Pa and 1-Pa.
       Distribution used (binomial or hypergeometric) is stated in the header.

Output — PNG (~/Downloads/):
       OC curve plot. If --aql / --rql provided, vertical lines annotate the plan's
       performance at those quality levels with exact Pa values.
       Filename: <datetime>_jrc_as_oc_curve.png

Interpretation guidance:
       The Pa at AQL is the producer's confidence: 1 - Pa(AQL) is the producer's
       risk — the probability of rejecting a conforming lot.
       The Pa at RQL is the consumer's risk: probability of accepting a bad lot.
       A well-designed plan targets Pa(AQL) >= 0.95 and Pa(RQL) <= 0.10.
       Use jrc_as_attributes to design a plan satisfying specific risk targets.

Example:
       jrc_as_oc_curve 32 1
       jrc_as_oc_curve 32 1 --aql 0.01 --rql 0.10
       jrc_as_oc_curve 50 2 --lot-size 200 --aql 0.025 --rql 0.10
